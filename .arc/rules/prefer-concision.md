Convey the essential idea once and concisely; do not over-explain. Flag comments or documentation that restate what the code already makes plain, narrate the obvious, or justify a self-evident invariant at length — and exhaustive or oddly-specific examples offered where the general idea already carries the point. Padding is not free: each redundant clause or illustrative specific is surface that must itself be kept accurate as the code and world change, and it dilutes the signal it was meant to add. Prefer names and structure that make explanation unnecessary over prose that compensates for their absence.
