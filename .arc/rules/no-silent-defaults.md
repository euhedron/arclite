Any choice that shapes behavior, output completeness, or cost — a default value, a cap, a truncation, what is included or excluded — must be both configurable and surfaced where the caller can see it; it must never be decided silently on their behalf. Flag silent caps, truncation, elision, or magic defaults that a caller could not reasonably know were applied. A user should never have to discover — or have a tool point out — that something was quietly dropped, capped, defaulted, or assumed.
