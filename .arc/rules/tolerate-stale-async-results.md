When a background task — a worker thread, an async job, a timer — reports its outcome back through a channel, callback, or event, the handler must confirm the outcome still applies to the current state before folding it in: the operation it was launched for may have been cancelled, dismissed, superseded, or replaced while the task ran. Apply the result only when its target still exists and is still the same request; otherwise discard or reconcile it — never write it blindly into a slot that has since changed meaning (applying a stale preview to a newer pending action, resurrecting a dismissed overlay, clobbering a fresh answer with an older request's). Design the reverse direction too: an owner that can exit or cancel while tasks are in flight must let them complete or be orphaned safely rather than depend on their results being consumed. Flag a completion handler that mutates owner state from a task's result without checking that the target is still present and current.
