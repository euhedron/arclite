A check that reports a safety, currency, or health verdict — whether a newer version exists, whether anything is vulnerable, whether something is valid, current, or clean — must not let a failed or inconclusive run collapse into the reassuring affirmative. When a caller relies on the verdict to decide or to gate, a network, auth, or I/O failure must propagate as an error, never be mapped to "up to date", "nothing found", or "passes": a falsely reassuring negative is worse than a loud failure, because it hides exactly the condition the check exists to surface. Only a genuinely advisory, non-blocking use — an optional hint nothing depends on — may degrade to a benign default on failure, and that degradation must be a deliberate, documented choice at the call site rather than an error swallowed in passing. Flag a status or safety check whose error path resolves "could not determine" into the positive, all-clear outcome.
