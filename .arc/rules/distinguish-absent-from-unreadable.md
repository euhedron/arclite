A thing that is absent and a thing that is present-but-unreadable are different outcomes and must not collapse into one. A read, list, or load of something that simply isn't there should resolve to a benign empty result the caller interprets, while a permission, I/O, parse, or corruption failure on something that does exist must propagate as a real error — never be swallowed into "nothing there yet." Flag any read, list, or load whose error handling maps every failure into the empty case: that lets a dropped or corrupt resource masquerade as a clean initial state and hides genuine failures from the caller.
