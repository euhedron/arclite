A rule must earn its place by the general principle it captures, applicable on its own merits — not because it would catch some specific attribute of the codebase at hand. Flag a rule whose reason for existing reads as reverse-engineered from a particular thing it would snag rather than stated as an independently general standard. Being mechanically checkable, or something a plain linter could also catch, is not itself a defect — standard, lower-judgment smells are legitimate rules when the underlying principle is general; the test is the generality and value of the principle, never whether it demands sophisticated judgment.
